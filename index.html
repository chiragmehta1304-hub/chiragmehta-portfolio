📊 MARKET ANALYSIS

Ethereum's Fee Revenue Collapsed 80% — Is the L2 Thesis Eating Its Own Parent?

Dencun's blob transactions solved scalability but created an identity crisis for Ethereum's business model.

Ethereum's base layer fee revenue has cratered since the Dencun upgrade, dropping over 80% as L2s migrated to cheap blob transactions. On the face of it, this looks catastrophic. But the bear case misses Ethereum's bet: it isn't monetizing base layer execution — it's monetizing the settlement layer beneath an entire rollup economy.

The real metric to watch isn't L1 fees; it's blob fee revenue growth and the number of L2s using Ethereum as their data availability layer. Arbitrum, Base, and Optimism collectively processed more transactions in Q1 2025 than Ethereum did in all of 2023. The pie is bigger — just distributed differently.

🌍 MACRO × CRYPTO

The Fed Held Rates Again. Here's Why That's Quietly Bullish for Crypto in Q3

Connecting the dots between Powell's cautious pivot language and what history says about crypto performance in rate plateau environments.

The Federal Reserve held the federal funds rate at 5.25–5.50% for the fifth consecutive meeting. Markets yawned. But for crypto traders, the subtext matters more than the headline. When the Fed enters a prolonged pause — not cutting, but also done hiking — risk assets historically enter a quiet accumulation phase before the next leg up.

Look at 2019: BTC rallied 170% in a five-month window during a Fed pause that preceded rate cuts. The dynamic isn't identical today, but the structure rhymes. Global M2 money supply has ticked up 3.2% over the past quarter, and dollar liquidity conditions — a historically reliable crypto price correlate — are loosening at the margins.

⬡ DEFI DEEP-DIVE

Aave v3's Efficiency Mode Is Quietly Reshaping How DeFi Manages Collateral Risk

A technical breakdown of how eMode works, who's using it, and why it matters for the future of collateral-efficient lending.

Aave v3's Efficiency Mode (eMode) allows users to borrow against correlated assets at loan-to-value ratios up to 97% — a number that would have sounded reckless in DeFi summer 2021. The key insight: if your collateral and debt are pegged to the same underlying (e.g., stETH and ETH), liquidation risk drops dramatically because they move together.

Since launch, eMode has generated over $2.1B in utilization with a liquidation rate 4x lower than standard borrowing pools, per Dune Analytics. This isn't just a protocol optimization — it's a shift in how DeFi thinks about risk-adjusted capital efficiency, and it's a template other protocols are beginning to copy.

📊 SECTOR REPORT

State of the Restaking Sector: $15B TVL, Three Dominant Protocols, and an Emerging Systemic Risk

A structured overview of EigenLayer, Symbiotic, and Karak — where the money is, where the risk hides, and what happens if things go wrong.

Restaking has grown from a conceptual whitepaper idea to a $15B+ sector in under 18 months. The premise is elegant: use already-staked ETH to secure additional protocols, earning layered yield. The risk is less elegant — a slashing event on one Actively Validated Service (AVS) could theoretically cascade through the underlying ETH stake.

EigenLayer holds 68% market share with $10.2B TVL but has yet to enable live AVS slashing in production. That's a critical nuance: current restaking TVL is generating yield without carrying the actual risk it's supposed to be priced for. When slashing goes live, we'll find out if the market has priced risk correctly — or significantly underpriced it.
